Brigadeiro

Description

Brigadeiro is a traditional Brazilian sweet that holds a special place in the countryâ€™s culinary culture. Originating in the 1940s, brigadeiro is made from a simple mix of condensed milk, butter, and cocoa powder. The ingredients are cooked together until the mixture reaches a thick, creamy consistency. Once cooled, this mixture is rolled into small balls and coated with chocolate sprinkles, creating a delightful contrast between the crunchy exterior and the soft, sweet interior.

In addition to its irresistible flavor, brigadeiro is a staple at Brazilian parties and celebrations, from birthdays to more casual gatherings. Its popularity has even extended beyond Brazil's borders, earning international recognition. Versatile by nature, brigadeiro can be found in various flavors, including coffee, passion fruit, and even gourmet versions with sophisticated ingredients. The simplicity of its recipe and the pleasure derived from its rich, sweet taste make brigadeiro a true symbol of Brazilian cuisine.

ingredients

  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk
  • 3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa
  • 1 tablespoon butter

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Combine condensed milk, cocoa, and butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Cook and stir until thickened, about 10 minutes. Remove from heat and let rest until mixture is cool enough to handle.
  3. Use your hands to shape into small balls and place on a serving plate. Can be eaten at once or chilled until serving.
